Animal Farm Book Report

1. General Information

Report Cover for "Animal Farm":

  • Title: "Animal Farm"
  • Author's Name: George Orwell
  • Genre: Allegorical novella, Political Satire
  • Year of Publication: 1945
  • Publisher: Secker and Warburg

1.2 Summarize the Book (up to 120 words)

Animal Farm by George Orwell tells the story of farm animals rebelling against their human farmer, dreaming of creating a fair society. However, the pigs who lead the rebellion become corrupt, mirroring human flaws. The book warns about the dangers of corrupt leadership and the idealism in political movements, exploring how good intentions can turn into oppression and manipulation. In conclusion, it's about the abuse of power and political ideas.

1.3 Author's Biography and Critical Reception

Author's Biography: George Orwell, born in India in 1903, was a British writer known for "Animal Farm" and "Nineteen Eighty-Four." He supported democratic socialism and opposed totalitarianism. Orwell died in 1950 (London).

Critical Reception of "Animal Farm": Animal Farm is praised for its clear portrayal of political ideas, with critiques of the Russian Revolution. It's now a literature classic, studied for its insights into power, corruption, and totalitarianism.

2. Book Plot

2.1 Exposition: Characters, Setting, Narrator, and Perspective

Characters: Old Major, Napoleon, Snowball, Boxer, Squealer.

Setting: Manor Farm, renamed Animal Farm after the rebellion.

Narrator and Perspective: Third-person narrator, revealing thoughts and actions like a detailed movie.

2.2 Action and Development of the Plot

  • Rising Actions: Old Major's speech inspires rebellion, animals take over.
  • Climax: Power struggle between Napoleon and Snowball.
  • Falling Action: Napoleon becomes authoritarian, animals realize broken promises.
  • Interpretation: "Animal Farm" is a cautionary tale about leaders forgetting promises and the gap between ideals and reality.

3. Opinion

Title: Unveiling My Perspective on "Animal Farm"

In my opinion, George Orwell's "Animal Farm" presented a bit of a challenge in terms of understanding, but despite that, it has a really interesting plot. The concept of rebellion within the narrative is well done, and the story's evolution from the animals' uprising to the intricate development of leadership was intriguing for me. Though the reading initially seemed to demand more attention, each twist in the story added layers of intrigue and revelation, making me read more. Moreover, in my reading experience, the characters, particularly Napoleon and Snowball, became two symbolic characters that carried profound messages about authority and its potential for corruption. The twists and turns in the plot, as seen through their actions, kept me engaged. In summary, I think that the plot of "Animal Farm" is literary perfect. Its ability to blend the simplicity of the farm with the complexities of politics creates a unique reading. Furthermore, the narrative's ability to entertain while offering a unique perspective on authority and corruption
